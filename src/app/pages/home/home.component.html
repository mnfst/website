Why CASE ?

Because apps take time to develop, and a lot of that time is spent in repetitive work. Moreover, building an app requires several talents combined. It is not common to see a good developer being a good UX designer too.

CASE focuses on CRUD apps: custom web apps like internal tools, ERPs, CRMs, admin panels and dashboards. Apps where the Create-Read-Update-Delete features have a leading role.

Its like an ORM on steroids

An ORM modelizes the objects or entities in your app to manipulate them without worrying about their database storage. CASE pushes further that concept by generating the UI from the description of your entities and their properties. You describe your data and the application adapts itself to it.

CASE is based on TypeORM. If you are used to ORMs like TypeORM, Prisma or Sequelize, it would be a breeze to create your CASE app.

The holy trinity of views:
list , create/edit & detail

Most of the CRUD apps follow the same view structure.

CASE follows that pattern by giving you a rich set of elements for the 3 available views.

What is the compromise ?

Using CASE and thus saving a huge amount of time obviously comes with customization limitations. Up to you to judge if it is a good match for your project.

CASE offers you:
  • A nice UI for your lists, create/edit views and detail views
  • To choose from a rich list of form elements to collect data (inputs) and to display it (yields)
  • To hook your own custom logic (along with third-party packages) to entity events (onCreate, onDelete)
CASE is NOT for you if:
  • Your app workflow does not follow the list-create/edit-detail pattern
  • Your app needs a lot of customisation, either on the elements, UI or the behavior

As a developper, you should prefer CASE over other market solutions.

CASE VS app builder, no-code, low-code

You do not need to be babysitted with drag-and-drop options and visual database builders.

CASE VS lib, packages, frameworks, boilerplates

They inevitably end up adding complexity to your stack, repelling developers and increasing technical debt.
